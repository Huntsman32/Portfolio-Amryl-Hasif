Spiderman, An Extraordinary Story

Spiderverse, The variations of spidermen


When I watched Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse yesterday, I had uncertainties about what to expect. It had been five years since the amazing Into The Spider-Verse film was released, and that movie completely amazed me. However, with the passage of time and the impact of the pandemic, I couldn't help but be cautious about sequels as they have let me down in the past. As it turned out, my worries were unnecessary. As the movie came to an end and the credits started rolling, the theater erupted in audible gasps. One person even exclaimed, "What!?" and the entire audience broke into applause. It was an extraordinary reaction from the crowd, and you will understand why once you see it.


Across The Spider-Verse takes place some time after the events of the first film. Miles, now older and feeling down, longs for his Spider-friends who returned to their own universes after defeating Fisk and seemingly resolving the multi-verse issue. However, it turns out that the multi-verse problems were far from over. The sequel reveals the existence of numerous holes that allow villains and monsters to cross between universes. To address this, a society of Spider-people forms with the goal of patching these holes. The concept of holes plays a significant role in the film, even tying into one of its villains, Spot. Overall, the sequel explores the continued challenges posed by the multi-verse and introduces new characters and conflicts for Miles and the Spider-people to face.
